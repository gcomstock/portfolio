## Problem Definition

<ColGrid>
  <ColGridItem image={imgProbBroken} title="Observability at Netflix Is Fragmented">
    <p>Jobs to be done span different products with different UX patterns and little inter-operability. Some are actively maintained while others languish from underfunding.</p>
  </ColGridItem>
  <ColGridItem image={imgProbFragmented} title="Sometimes an External Vendor Works Out…">
    <p>The Observability team can't build everything. A new strategy bet for live streaming can't wait a year for internal tooling to catch up, so orgs are encouraged to sometimes buy a solution.</p>
  </ColGridItem>
  <ColGridItem image={imgProbVendor} title="…Often Times Not">
    <p>External tools add fragmentation and grow cost-prohibitive at Netflix's scale. Support becomes complicated, and the internal Observability team ends up chasing a vendor's full offering with a small fraction of the resources.</p>
  </ColGridItem>
</ColGrid>

<section id="pivot">

## Pivot

<ColGrid>
  <ColGridItem media={<SpectrumChart />} title="Broaden the Appeal">
    <p>We over-indexed on the novice troubleshooter because their feedback on the legacy products were strongest. More advanced queries across multiple applications were simply more difficult to do in the new UI. We needed a solution that appealed to both novice and power users.</p>
  </ColGridItem>
  <ColGridItem media={<GanttChart />} title="Stagger the Release">
    <p>The end goal was clear: a unified MELT experience and the deprecation of multiple legacy products. Delivering on all fronts was a stacked bet. We instead decided to stagger each feature release, starting with metrics, followed quickly by logs.</p>
  </ColGridItem>
  <ColGridItem media={<FeedbackFlow />} title="Fix the Pain Points">
    <p>Through customer validation sessions, UserVoice tickets, and a dedicated early feedback channel, we gathered and organized customer requests. Addressing the strongest signals became the highest priority.</p>
  </ColGridItem>
</ColGrid>
